## 2. The Spindle & Pulley Replacement Workflow
### Pre-Installation Checklist
Safety: Disconnect spark plug wires to prevent accidental startup.
Deck Prep: Scrape the underside of the Velocity Plus deck to bare metal. This ensures maximum airflow and prevents the new spindles from seating on debris.
Inspection: Check the deck reinforcement plate for "mushrooming" or cracks before mounting new hardware.
### Installation Tips & Tricks
The "Anti-Seize" Rule: Apply a thin layer of anti-seize to the spindle shaft before sliding the pulley on. This prevents future seizing.
Woodruff Key Alignment: Use a dab of grease to hold the woodruff key in place during pulley installation to prevent it from tipping.
Torque Pattern: Tighten spindle-to-deck bolts in a star pattern to 30-35 ft-lbs to ensure a perfectly level mounting.
Pulley Torque: Torque the top pulley nut to 55-65 ft-lbs.
### Critical Measurements
Pulley Height: Measure from the deck shell to the pulley rim to ensure the belt remains co-planar across all three spindles.
Blade Tracking: After installation, rotate blades so tips meet; they should be within 1/8" of each other in height.
Deck Pitch: Ensure the front of the deck is 1/8" to 1/4" lower than the rear for optimal discharge.
## 3. General Machine Maintenance Schedule
While the deck is serviced, perform these high-priority engine and drive system tasks:
Engine Oil & Filter: Change every 100 hours.
Fuel Filter: Replace every 100 hours.
Hydraulic System: Replace hydraulic oil and filters every 500 hours.
Tire Pressure: Maintain 12 PSI (Rear) and 25 PSI (Front) for a level cut.
Belt Tension: Check drive belts for "checking" or cracks. The belt should deflect 1/2" with 10 lbs of pressure.
Note: Always consult your specific Scag Illustrated Parts Manual for model-year specific part numbers (48", 52", or 61" decks).
