## PART IV: FORMAL MATHEMATICS
### Petri Nets for Non-Linear Stories
Petri nets are mathematical tools for modeling distributed systems with parallel processes.

Components:

Places (circles) = conditions or states (e.g., "Seeker is at Sacred Grove")
Transitions (bars) = actions or story beats (e.g., "Card draw triggers event")
Tokens (dots) = active markers (e.g., "current players in the system")
Arcs (arrows) = preconditions and consequences

Why Petri nets for narrative?

Unlike finite-state machines (FSM), Petri nets handle parallel storytelling. Multiple story threads can unfold simultaneously:

Seeker's personal arc (child's discovery)
Guide's personal arc (parent's witnessing)
Shared convergence moments (both at same node)

All three happen in parallel without bottlenecking into a single sequence.

V1.0 Implementation: Map the 12-episode arc as a Petri net where:

Each card = a transition
Each story beat = a place
Player state (tokens) determines which next transitions are available
