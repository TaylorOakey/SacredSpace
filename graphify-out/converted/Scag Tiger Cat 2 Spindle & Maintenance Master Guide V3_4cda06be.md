# Scag Tiger Cat 2: Complete Spindle, Pulley, and Maintenance Master Guide
Line Spacing: 1.15
## 1. Required Tools & Supplies
To perform this overhaul professionally, ensure you have the following tools on hand:
Sockets & Wrenches: 1/2", 9/16", 5/8", and 3/4" (Standard and Metric sets recommended).
Torque Wrench: Essential for Blade Bolts (75-100 ft-lbs) and Pulley Nuts (55-65 ft-lbs).
Impact Wrench: 1/2" drive (cordless or pneumatic) for removing stubborn deck hardware.
Pulley Removal Hardware: Two Grade 8 hex head bolts matching the thread of the pulley hub holes (the "Bolt Trick").
Maintenance Supplies:
High-Temp Lithium Grease (Scag Premium Spindle Grease p/n 486258).
Anti-Seize Lubricant (for spindle shafts).
Wire Brush and Heavy-Duty Putty Knife (for deck cleaning).
## 2. The Spindle & Pulley Replacement Process
### Pre-Removal Measurements
Before removing old units, record these distances to ensure factory-spec alignment:
Pulley Deck Height: Measure from the top of the deck shell to the top of the pulley rim.
Blade-to-Deck Gap: Measure from the blade tip to the bottom edge of the deck rim.

## 3. Comprehensive Maintenance & Lubrication
While the deck is disassembled, address the following service points per the Scag Operator's Manual:
### The "Hidden" Grease Map
New Spindles: Grease immediately until you feel resistance or see a tiny weep.
Caster Yokes: Grease the vertical shafts on the front wheels (every 40-100 hours).
Pump Drive Idler: Located under the seat; ensure the pivot arm moves freely.
Deck Lift Pivot: Grease the main cross-shaft to keep height adjustments smooth.
### General Machine Health
Engine Oil: Change every 100 hours (10w-30 or 15w-40).
Hydro System: Check expansion tanks. If oil is "Chocolate Milk" or "Black," change filters and fluid (typically 500-hour interval).
Tire Pressure: Set Rears to 12 PSI and Fronts to 25 PSI. This is vital for a level cut.
## 4. Final Validation Checklist
Blade Tracking: Rotate blades so tips meet. They must be within 1/8" of each other.
Deck Pitch: Ensure the front of the deck is 1/8" to 1/4" lower than the rear.
Belt Tension: Verify the belt deflects 1/2" under 10 lbs of pressure.
Hot Check: After 15 minutes of mowing, verify spindle housings are warm but not excessively hot.
