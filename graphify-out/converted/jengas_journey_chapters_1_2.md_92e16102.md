# JENGA'S JOURNEY
## Season One: The Awakening of the Arcana Adept
Canon Seal: @SOURCE Phase: Nigredo I — Nigredo II



A note to the artist: The Construct Realm lives in right angles, compression, and fluorescent light. The Living Realm breathes. Every panel that crosses from one to the other should feel like a different atmosphere entering the lungs. Jenga's eyes are the story's most important recurring image — they must be drawn with full attention in every close-up. We are going to lose them. The reader should not know this yet. But you should.


# CHAPTER ONE: THE CITY THAT FORGETS

## PAGE 1 — Splash
The city at 2am. Aerial establishing shot. A perfect grid of light — the Construct Realm in its most nakedly geometric form. Streets like circuitry. Buildings like stacked rectangles of function. The sky: orange-pink with light pollution, no stars visible anywhere.

Somewhere in the grid, barely visible from this altitude: a single figure. A wall beside it. A color on the wall that has no business being in this city.

CAPTION: "The City does not dream. It does not rest. It runs its programs through the night."

CAPTION: "Everything has a use. Everything has a price. Everything has a surveillance camera."

CAPTION: "Everything except the thing on the wall at the corner of Marcus and 9th."


## PAGE 2 — 2 Panels
Panel 1: Street level. The wall itself — the side of a municipal utility building, gray concrete, pre-tagged with faded official notices about zoning and ordinances. The mural is already more than halfway finished: forest green, emerald, root-brown, amber. We can't see what it depicts in full — just the scale and the color. It is 15 feet wide, 8 feet tall. The contrast with the surrounding gray is not decorative. It is almost violent. A small generator hums somewhere offscreen. Jenga stands at the center of the work, arm raised, spray can in hand.

CAPTION: "He started with the green. Always the green first."



Panel 2: Close on Jenga's face, three-quarter profile. 14 years old. His expression contains a quality of attention that doesn't quite fit the face of a teenager — focused, slightly wide-eyed, as if listening rather than creating. His eyes are his most specific physical feature: alert, direct, with something in them that looks like recognition. Like the wall is telling him something and he is writing back.

CAPTION: "He always said the image was already there. All he did was find it."


## PAGE 3 — 4 Panels
Panel 1: The mural taking shape. It has appeared without being consciously decided.

CAPTION: "He had been painting this same shape for five years."

CAPTION: "He didn't know why."

He takes a different route to the bus station. It is early morning — not dawn yet, that hour that belongs to no one. He rounds the corner at Marcus and 9th.

A note to the artist: This chapter is a descent into gravity. While the opening chapters existed in vertical space—aerial views and open horizons—Chapter Three is strictly horizontal. Keep the perspective low, pressed against the dirt. The visual language should feel heavier: emphasize the coarse texture of soil, the ridges of bark, and the unpolished grit of the natural world. Jenga is a city creature out of his element. His posture, his gear, his very movements should look mismatched against the landscape. The Grandmother, by contrast, is the landscape. That tension is our primary focus.
