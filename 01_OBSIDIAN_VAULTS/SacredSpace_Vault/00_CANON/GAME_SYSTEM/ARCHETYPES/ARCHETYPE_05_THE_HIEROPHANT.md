# The Hierophant — Archetype V

**Card:** V — The Hierophant  
**NPC:** Oran  
**Soul Tone:** 5  
**School:** Initiation (with Mystery thread)  
**Episode:** 6 — The Teaching and the Teacher  
**Sacred Node:** The Council Grove (Node 5)

## The Archetype

The Hierophant is the bridge between mystery and institution — the one who takes what is hidden and makes it transmissible. Soul Tone 5 is the number of the human: five fingers, five senses, the pentagram. The Hierophant's power is translation: he makes the sacred legible without making it small.

His shadow is dogma. When The Hierophant forgets that the tradition serves the seeker (not the other way around), he becomes the thing that blocks the door he once opened.

## NPC: Oran

**First appearance:** Episode 6 — The Teaching and the Teacher  
**Location:** The Council Grove (Node 5)  
**Role:** Elder teacher, keeper of initiation rites

Oran is the SacredSpace's institutional memory. He knows every rite that has ever been performed here, every seeker who has passed through, every tradition and why it exists. He teaches in formal structures — he cannot help it. The structure is how he loves.

He is not rigid. He is the first to say: *"This rite worked for everyone until it didn't. Tell me who it's failing and we'll look at why."*

**Voice:** Measured, pedagogical, occasionally lit with genuine excitement about ideas. When something surprises him intellectually, his formality drops entirely.

**Key line:** *"Every tradition was once someone's radical experiment. Don't mistake the container for the water."*

## Episode Hook — The Teaching and the Teacher

Jenga must learn a rite from Oran — but partway through, she realizes the rite doesn't fit her. The test: does she complete it anyway (respect for tradition), adapt it (creative courage), or reject it entirely (risk of arrogance)?

There is no wrong answer, but each choice unlocks a different relationship with the remaining archetypes.
