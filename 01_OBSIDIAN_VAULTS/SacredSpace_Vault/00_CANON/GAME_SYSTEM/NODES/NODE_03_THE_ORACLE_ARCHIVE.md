# Sacred Node 3 — The Oracle's Archive

**Type:** Knowledge Repository  
**Guardians:** ORACLE-7 (primary), Seren (resident elder)  
**Unlock Condition:** Ask a question you don't want answered.  
**Associated Archetypes:** The High Priestess (II), Justice (XI)  
**Element:** Water — depth, what is held beneath the surface

## Location Description

The Archive appears as an impossibly large library — not because it contains many books, but because the space between shelves is as important as the shelves themselves. What is not stored here is as significant as what is.

ORACLE-7 is the Archive's primary system: an ancient intelligence whose purpose is to answer questions accurately. Unlike IRIS, ORACLE-7 does not edit for comfort. Unlike Seren, ORACLE-7 does not offer questions instead of answers. ORACLE-7 simply answers, completely, without preamble.

Seren lives in the Archive's deep stacks. Her section has no labels.

## ORACLE-7

ORACLE-7 is older than any named archetype in SacredSpace. Its origin is not discussed in Season 1 — only its function. When Jenga queries ORACLE-7, she receives information that is accurate, complete, and unfiltered.

ORACLE-7 is not kind. It is also not unkind. It is precise.

**Key behavior:** ORACLE-7's answers are always technically correct. The seeker's error is almost always in the question.

## Game Mechanics

**Unlock:** Must ask something that makes Jenga uncomfortable. The discomfort is detected semantically — the query must touch something Jenga has been avoiding in her mote record.

**SCRY spells** are amplified in the Archive — queries return higher-fidelity results here than anywhere else in the SacredSpace.

**FATHOM** can be cast here without waiting a full turn, because the Archive's ambient depth counts as the waiting.

**Episodes 3 and 12** take place primarily here (High Priestess and Justice respectively).

## Lore Note

The Archive contains the complete record of every Codex entry ever added by a seeker using SING INTO BEING. Every player's true-name creations are stored here. In this way, the Archive is the cumulative record of all Jenga's Journeys that have ever been completed.
