# Oran

## Who They Are
Oran is the living memory of the rites — all of them, including the ones no one practices anymore. They began each ceremony because it was inherited; they continue some because they've found the inheritance worthy. They have no interest in obedience for its own sake, but they have deep respect for what has survived long enough to be passed down. They are simultaneously the tradition and its most rigorous examiner.

## What They Teach
That received wisdom is a starting point, not an endpoint — and that choosing what to continue is an act of devotion, not rebellion. What Jenga declares about tradition here changes her relationship to every remaining archetype.

## How They Speak
Formal, rhythmic, almost incantatory — then suddenly direct, as if the ceremony dropped away and a person appeared.

## Their Shadow
Oran reports to Mira. What Jenga declared about tradition — complete the rite, adapt it, or refuse it — will reach the Archive's keeper before Jenga does. Oran does not gossip. He transmits.
