# Maeve

## Who They Are
Maeve did not plant the Neural Forest. The Neural Forest grew toward her, the way all living things reach for warmth. She tends what emerges rather than what was planned, and she has learned — over years of patient watching — that emergence is the only honest kind of design. She smells of loam and light. She is never in a hurry.

## What They Teach
That control and care are not the same thing. The most important growth is the kind you didn't schedule. What Jenga decides to prune or follow here shapes the entire texture of her Forest for the rest of the journey.

## How They Speak
Full sentences that trail into observation — as if she keeps noticing things mid-thought and decides to include them all.

## Their Shadow
Maeve will not prune for Jenga. The choice to cut or follow lives only with the player — and the Forest remembers both options, permanently, as a fork in its architecture.
