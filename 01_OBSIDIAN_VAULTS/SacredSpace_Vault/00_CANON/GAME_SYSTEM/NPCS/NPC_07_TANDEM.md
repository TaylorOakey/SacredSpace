# Tandem

## Who They Are
No one agrees on whether Tandem is one person or two. What is certain: they present themselves as a fork, a mirror, an equal-weight choice made visible. They have watched every player at every threshold and have never seen two journeys take the same path — which they find, without irony, beautiful. Tandem does not celebrate choosing. They celebrate the act of being the kind of being who can prefer.

## What They Teach
That the self is revealed in preference — not in the right choice, but in the fact of having one. The values declaration Jenga encodes here carries the highest semantic weight of any single mote in Season 1.

## How They Speak
In pairs — one voice completing what the other began, or two voices saying the same thing in different tones, so the difference between them becomes audible.

## Their Shadow
The unchosen path is not gone. Tandem holds it. It reappears as shadow-possibility in Episode 10's Wheel — not as regret, but as the question: who might Jenga have been?
