#### PART 1: WHICH IRS FORM? (1023-N vs 1023-EZ vs 1023)

| Form | Best For | Timeline | Cost | Complexity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1023-N (New)** | Organizations expecting <$50k annual revenue in first 5 years | 2–4 weeks | $275 | Simple (8-page form) |
| **1023-EZ** | Very small orgs, <$10k gross revenue projected | 1–2 weeks | $275 | Minimal (3 pages) |
| **1023 (Full)** | Organizations expecting >$50k annual revenue | 90–120 days | $600 | Complex (30+ pages, detailed financials) |

**SacredSpace Recommendation: 1023-N**

Why: Your projected Year 1 is $50k–$100k (Kickstarter revenue, Open Grove tuition, SacredArcana royalties). This sits at the 1023-N ceiling. If conservative, you could use 1023-EZ; if ambitious, you'd need full 1023.

**Go with 1023-N.** It's the middle path: faster than full 1023 (2–4 weeks), more credible than 1023-EZ for your revenue tier, and IRS approval at 1023-N level is effectively "tax-exempt 501(c)(3) status" even if you later file a full 1023 to upgrade.

**Key Tax Consideration: UBTI (Unrelated Business Taxable Income)**

If SacredSpace 501(c)(3) receives royalties from SacredArcana Studios LLC POD sales, those royalties are **not UBTI** because:

- POD sales are tangential to mission (not the org's primary activity)
- Royalties are passive income (not the org actively running Etsy)
- Threshold: <$1k/year UBTI = no Form 990-T filing required

**If SacredSpace generated >$1k/year UBTI, you'd file Form 990-T (Unrelated Business Income Tax Return), but that's future-state and manageable.**

**For launch:** Keep the structures separate. SacredArcana LLC pays SacredSpace Sanctuary a percentage (20–30% suggested) of POD royalties as "mission support donation."
