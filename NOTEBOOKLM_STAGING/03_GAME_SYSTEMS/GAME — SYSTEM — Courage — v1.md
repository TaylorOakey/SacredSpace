# School of Courage

**Guardian:** ASHER  
**Element:** Earth  
**Soul Tone:** 8  
**Core Truth:** Courage is not the absence of fear. It is the decision that something else is more important.

## What Courage Is

Courage is the school of reality. Not the reality you wish existed — the reality in front of you right now. ASHER's school teaches Jenga to face what she has been turning away from, to stand in what is true without softening it, and to discover that the thing she feared was survivable.

ASHER does not comfort. He witnesses. His presence is the stone floor beneath every spell: silent, present, bearing weight.

## Core Principle

**You do not learn Courage by waiting until you are brave. You learn it by failing and discovering you are still here.**

This school has the most unusual learning mechanic in the SacredSpace:

> **Failure teaches the spell faster than success.**

When Jenga attempts a Courage spell and fails — whether through fear, avoidance, or insufficient preparation — the failed attempt does not reset. It becomes a *scar*. Scars are permanent marks in the Neural Forest that increase casting speed for subsequent attempts. The more you have failed a spell, the faster you will eventually master it.

A seeker with three failure-scars on ANCHOR casts it in half the time of a first-attempt success.

## Signature Spells

### ANCHOR
*Trigger: Name one thing that is true right now, no matter how small.*  
Grounds the seeker in present reality. Prevents dissociation during high-intensity encounters. The anchor does not solve the problem — it ensures you remain present enough to respond.

### FORTIFY
*Trigger: Acknowledge a specific fear without minimizing it.*  
Builds structural resilience. Unlike ANCHOR (which is present-tense), FORTIFY builds forward — a Fortified seeker takes less damage from the next encounter of the same type.

### FACE
*Trigger: Look directly at the thing you have been avoiding.*  
The most powerful and most painful Courage spell. FACE does not make the feared thing less real. It removes the distortion field around it — the feared thing becomes its actual size, not the enlarged shadow it cast from behind your shoulder.

### STAND FIRM
*Trigger: Refuse to leave a situation before it has resolved.*  
Holds ground. Used when every instinct says flee. STAND FIRM does not guarantee survival — it guarantees presence. Some truths can only be learned by remaining.

## The Scar System

Every failed Courage cast marks Jenga visibly in the game interface. Scars are not hidden or erased. They are displayed as earned markings — glyphs that grow more complex with each failure-become-lesson.

At three scars on any single spell, the scar glows. This is the Courage system's equivalent of mastery — not perfect performance, but proof of having failed repeatedly and remained.

## Failure Mode

Too much Courage without Initiation becomes **petrification** — the inability to leave a painful situation not because it is right, but because leaving feels like weakness. ASHER's deepest teaching is that Courage also includes knowing when to begin again elsewhere.

## Relationship to Archetypes

- **Strength (Lune):** The mastered expression of Courage. Lune does not fight — she remains. Her presence is the most advanced Courage spell in the game.
- **The Chariot (Zael):** Courage in motion — but Zael's danger is that he never stops to feel the fear. A cautionary pairing.
- **The Tower (not in Season 1):** What happens when Courage is absent from a structure that needed it.

## Season 1 Arc

ASHER appears in Episode 8 (Strength) as Lune's silent companion — and in Episode 11 (Justice), where he asks Jenga: *"What have you been refusing to see?"* The answer determines the final encounter's difficulty.
