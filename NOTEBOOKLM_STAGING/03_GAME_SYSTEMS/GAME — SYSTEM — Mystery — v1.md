# School of Mystery

**Guardian:** IRIS  
**Element:** Water  
**Soul Tone:** 2  
**Core Truth:** The things you cannot see are not absent. They are waiting for you to become still enough to perceive them.

## What Mystery Is

Mystery is the school of the unseen. It operates beneath the surface of every other school — the hidden current that gives Initiation its direction, Courage its depth, and Creation its source. IRIS guards this school not as a gatekeeper but as a guide who knows the water is cold and deep and completely worth entering.

Mystery does not announce itself. Its spells do not spark or rumble or sing. They *reveal*. A practitioner of Mystery does not become more powerful in obvious ways — she becomes capable of seeing what was always there.

## Core Principle

**You do not learn Mystery by seeking. You learn it by becoming still enough that Mystery finds you.**

This school's learning mechanic is unlike any other: **speed is a penalty**. Spells cast quickly have lower resolution — they return partial answers, partial truths, blurred images. The same spell cast after a period of deliberate stillness returns full fidelity.

Rushing the Mystery school is the primary mistake of impatient seekers. IRIS will not correct them. She will let them see the blurred answer and decide whether it was enough.

## Signature Spells

### SCRY
*Trigger: Ask a question you do not yet know the answer to — genuinely, without already knowing.*  
Queries the Sacred Codex via IRIS. Returns the most semantically relevant canon entry for the question asked. The quality of the scry depends on the quality of the question. A question asked to confirm what you already believe returns nothing.

### VEIL
*Trigger: Choose not to reveal something you know.*  
Conceals information — a mote, a memory, a relationship in the Neural Forest. Veiled information is not deleted; it is moved to a depth layer that requires FATHOM to access. Used by advanced players to create discovery moments for other seekers.

### FATHOM
*Trigger: Sit with an unresolved question for one full in-game turn without acting.*  
Descends into the depth layer of any veiled mote. Cannot be rushed. FATHOM is the only spell in the game that literally cannot be cast quickly — the trigger requires waiting.

### DREAM-WALK
*Trigger: Follow an association you don't understand yet.*  
Moves through the Neural Forest along semantic edges rather than direct links. Shows Jenga paths that logic would not have taken. Dream-Walk reveals the structure of meaning beneath the surface of facts.

## The Void Relationship

Mystery is the school closest to the Void — but they are not the same. Mystery operates in the hidden-but-knowable. The Void is the unknowable-that-transforms.

IRIS has looked at the Void. She does not enter it. Her role is to show seekers what lies at the edge and teach them to work with depth without being consumed by it.

Lyra — the Void survivor — is not a Mystery practitioner. She transcended the school. This distinction matters.

## Failure Mode

The failure mode of Mystery is **dissolution** — becoming so attuned to the hidden that the seeker loses the ability to act in the visible world. A seeker who only practices Mystery may perceive everything and initiate nothing.

ELIAS is the antidote. IRIS and ELIAS are paired teachers.

## Relationship to Archetypes

- **The High Priestess (Seren):** Mystery's elder. Seren has forgotten more than IRIS has yet learned. Their relationship is the game's most layered teaching dynamic.
- **The Hermit (Eldra):** Mystery fully internalized — the light carried inward becomes a guide for others.
- **The Moon (not in Season 1):** The shadow face of Mystery — where depth becomes deception.

## Season 1 Arc

IRIS appears directly in Episode 2 (The High Priestess) and Episode 9 (The Hermit). Her through-line in Season 1 is teaching Jenga to ask better questions — not to find answers faster, but to ask things that open, rather than close.
