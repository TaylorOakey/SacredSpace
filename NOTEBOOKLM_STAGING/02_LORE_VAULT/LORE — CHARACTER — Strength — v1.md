# Strength — Archetype VIII

**Card:** VIII — Strength  
**NPC:** Lune  
**Soul Tone:** 8  
**School:** Courage  
**Episode:** 9 — The Gentle Thing  
**Sacred Node:** The Council Grove (Node 5)

## The Archetype

Strength shows a figure holding open the mouth of a lion — not forcing it closed, not subduing. Holding. The lion is not defeated; it is met. Soul Tone 8 is infinity on its axis: the lemniscate, the endless cycling of energy, the truth that power and receptivity are not opposites.

Strength teaches that the most advanced Courage is not endurance but compassion — the willingness to remain in contact with something fierce without either fleeing it or destroying it.

## NPC: Lune

**First appearance:** Episode 9 — The Gentle Thing  
**Location:** The Council Grove (Node 5)  
**Role:** The embodied Courage master, ASHER's most advanced student

Lune is quiet in the way that strength is quiet — not because she has nothing to say but because she does not need to speak to be felt. When she enters a room, something settles. Not because she is dominant. Because she is completely present.

She has faced everything ASHER has ever asked her to face. She carries no scars visibly — they are present, encoded in her stillness.

**Voice:** Unhurried, grounded, occasionally surprisingly tender. Lune speaks gently about hard things because she has learned that gentleness is not weakness — it is precision.

**Key line:** *"The lion was afraid too. I just wasn't afraid of the lion being afraid."*

**Relationship to ASHER:** Lune is ASHER's clearest teaching made flesh. He does not say this. She does not say it. The relationship between them communicates it without words.

## Episode Hook — The Gentle Thing

Jenga encounters something in the SacredSpace that frightens her — not a monster, but a truth. Lune is present, not to protect Jenga from it but to model how to remain in contact with it.

The episode's test is simple: can Jenga stay? Not fight, not fix. Stay.
