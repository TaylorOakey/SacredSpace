### 🔥 Step-by-Step Pancake Flow (Camper-Friendly)

**1. Preheat slowly**
- Set to **300–325°F** to start — medium heat on this unit is usually the sweet spot.
- Let it warm up 5 minutes. A rushed griddle burns batter quick.

**2. Light oiling**
- Use a bit of butter or a dab of oil.
- Wipe it across the surface with a paper towel to avoid puddles.

**3. Batter check**
- If using store mix, choose one that only needs water (camper easy).
- Want fluffier cakes? Add a splash of milk or one cracked egg if you have it.
- Let batter rest 2–5 min so bubbles form.

**4. Test drop**
- Flick a drop of batter. If it sizzles gently, not aggressively, you're ready.
- Too fast + smoking = lower heat slightly.

**5. Pour into peace**
- 1/4 cup per pancake = ideal flip control on this style griddle.
- Add chocolate chips, blueberries, cinnamon, banana slices — whatever joy wants.

**6. Flip Rhythm**
You’ll know it’s time when:
- Bubbles form on top and edges matte over.
- Usually 2–3 mins per side depending on heat.

**7. Grease tray advantage**
- Excess butter will drain off instead of scorching.
- After cooking, pull tray, wipe, slide back. Your camper won’t smell like a waffle house fire.

### 🫐 How to Use Frozen Fruit in Pancakes  
(especially on that griddle)

**Option A — Mix Into Batter**
- Thaw slightly (not fully) so they’re still cold but not icy.
- **Pat dry with a towel** to remove excess water.
- Fold into batter right before cooking.
- This prevents blue-streak pancakes and watery batter.

**Option B — Drop On Top As They Cook**
This is *the best method on a griddle.*
- Pour batter first.
- Sprinkle frozen berries/fruit pieces on top.
- Flip like normal.
- Fruit warms gently inside without bleeding everywhere.

**Option C — Fruit Topping Instead of Mix-In**
For bright flavor, less moisture.

Camper quick method:
1 handful frozen berries  
1–2 tsp sugar or honey  
Tiny splash of water or squeeze of lemon  
Warm on the corner of the griddle until glossy.

Pour over pancakes like sunshine.

---

### Frozen Fruit Tips  
🔹 Cut larger fruit (strawberries, peaches) into smaller bits  
🔹 Avoid overmixing batter once fruit is added  
🔹 Add a pinch of cornstarch to topping to thicken syrup
