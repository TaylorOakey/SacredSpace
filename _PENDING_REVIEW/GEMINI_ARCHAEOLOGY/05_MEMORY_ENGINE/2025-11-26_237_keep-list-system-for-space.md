# **The Four Kingdoms of Home**
Think of your space as a living map:

| Kingdom | What Lives Here | How It Moves |
|---|---|---|
| **Body** | Clothing, hygiene, linens | Clean ↔ In-Use ↔ Dirty cycle |
| **Mind/Craft** | Art supplies, books, instruments | Stored ↔ Active ↔ Showcase |
| **Heart** | Sentimental items, mementos | Archive ↔ Altar ↔ Rotation |
| **Power** | Electronics, tools, cords | Charged ↔ Ready ↔ Retire/Recycle |

We’ll detail each kingdom below.

---

## 1. **Clothing: A Three-Stage Flow**
Let garments behave like seasons migrating across the land.

### **Dirty → In-Between → Fresh**
- **Dirty:** hamper or bin separated by *lights/darks/towels* or *daily/outerwear/work clothes*
- **In-Between:** a dedicated **“not dirty enough to wash / not clean enough to drawer”** rail or hook
  - Example: 3 hooks labeled  
    **OUT-ONCE | LIGHTWEAR | OUTDOOR/HEAVY**
- **Clean:**  
  - **Daily Wear** in top drawer  
  - **Work/Outdoor** separate zone  
  - **Seasonal/Occasional** boxed or high shelf  

### Weekly Ritual
🔄 Sort → Wash → Fold → Return  
Like returning chess pieces to their square.

---

## 2. **Art Supplies: Accessible Creativity**
Your muse shouldn’t be locked behind lids.

### Three-tier access:
- **Tier 1: Always-Ready**  
  Cup/jar for pencils, pens, scissors. Tabletop.
- **Tier 2: Project-Baskets**  
  One basket/bin per type of creation  
  *(painting, clay, sewing, digital, collage)*
- **Tier 3: Deep Storage**  
  Bulk supplies, seasonal mediums, backups  
  *labeled, airtight, stacked*

### Optional flourish:
🎨 **A rolling art-altar cart**  
It follows you like a loyal familiar.

---

## 3. **Sentimental Items: Heart-Memory System**
These deserve reverence, not random drawers.

### Two Realms:
- **Sacred Altar (on display)**  
  A shelf for rotating treasures: photos, gifts, relics of your story.
- **The Archive Box (kept)**  
  Beautifully labeled, fireproof if possible.  
  - Subdividers: *Childhood, Family, Love Letters, Achievements, Milestones*

### Monthly Touch-In
Choose one archived item to bring out, remember, honor, and return or replace.

---

## 4. **Electronics & Tech**
Prevent the “cord-nest dread.” Make a tech dojo.

### Zones:
📡 **Charging Shrine** – phones, batteries, tablets  
🧰 **Active Drawer** – tools, adapters, USBs, SSDs  
📦 **Deep-Store Tech** – old drives, replacement parts, cables sorted by type  
🕊 **Recycle/Repurpose Box** – anything unused 90+ days goes here

Label cords like they are friendly spirits:
- Phone USB-C
- Camera charger
- Game controller
- Laptop brick

A cheap labeler is a magical upgrade.

Limitations are like riverbanks — they don’t restrict the water, they **give it direction**. Here’s a sculpted list of boundaries designed to focus energy, reduce overwhelm, and keep SacredSpace growth sustainable, embodied, and joyful.

---

## **STRUCTURAL LIMITATIONS**
These shape *how* we build.

- No system may exist without a **home + cycle + reset ritual**
- Max **3 active projects** at any time
- New idea only enters after **one older idea is completed or archived**
- No storage container without a **label + category + purpose**
- Physical + digital spaces must **mirror** one another in organization

---

## **TIME-BASED LIMITATIONS**
To ensure momentum without burnout.

- Daily creation window **(30–90 mins)** — non-negotiable
- Weekly review only — no revisiting decisions made earlier in the week
- Once a month: **purge + simplify**
- No more than **2 hours of setup** before action begins
- 24-hour buffer before major purchases or new equipment

---

## **CLUTTER & MINIMALISM LIMITATIONS**
This keeps space clean and intentional.

- If it hasn’t been used in **60 days**, it gets archived, gifted, or repurposed
- **Sentimental items** must fit within their designated box — no overflow
- Incoming clothes must match or replace one outgoing item
- No junk drawer — only categorized micro-zones
- Cords must be labeled or they **don’t stay**

---

## **CREATIVE LIMITATIONS**
To sharpen artistry rather than scatter it.

- Use what you already have before buying anything new
- Max **two mediums** per active art project
- No project without a **clear Definition of Done**
- Sketch first — build second — expand last
- Quantity creates quality, but **quality shapes legacy**

---

## **ORGANIZATION LIMITATIONS**
To support workflow flow and mental spaciousness.

- No horizontal stacks — only vertical or container-based
- Every item must be reachable in **two motions or less**
- Clean surfaces in the morning = clean mind for the day
- **No hidden piles** — visible systems only
- When in doubt: **reduce to essence**

---

## **ENERGY & WELLBEING LIMITATIONS**
To honor the body and spirit that fuels creation.

- No all-day chaos — 3 tidy resets: *morning, mid-day, night*
- Work stops when creativity turns to frustration
- Rest counts as progress
- Only keep objects that feel alive, useful, or meaningful
- Nothing enters the SacredSpace without intention
