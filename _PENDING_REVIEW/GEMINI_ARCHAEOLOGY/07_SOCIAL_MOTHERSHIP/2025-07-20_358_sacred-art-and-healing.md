---

### **Title: "A Sacred Space: The Path of Jenga"**

---

### **Main Characters:**

- **Jenga (real name: Malik or Javier)**  
  A gifted but troubled city teen. His graffiti is more than rebellion—it's sacred geometry, symbols, and visual poetry he's intuitively channeling without knowing why.

- **Grandma (Abuela/Sipatu)**  
  A respected medicine woman, keeper of ancient traditions. She communicates with spirits, plants, and animals. She sees Jenga's gift as something sacred that needs guidance.

- **Ayla (or Nia)**  
  A young tribal apprentice chosen by the spirits to be a future healer. Wise beyond her years, she's deeply connected to the jungle and its mystical energies, but curious about the outside world.

---

### **Plot Arcs / Chapters:**

#### **1. Urban Jungle**
- Introduce Jenga in the city: alienation, graffiti art, run-ins with police.
- His tags have hidden power—mysterious things happen near his murals.
- After getting caught, the judge gives him a choice: juvenile hall or live with his grandmother in a remote jungle.

#### **2. The Green Exile**
- Jenga arrives in the jungle, totally out of his element.
- At first, he hates it—no WiFi, bugs, strange rituals.
- Starts having vivid dreams and visions he can’t explain.

#### **3. Ayla’s World**
- Meets Ayla—initially they don’t get along.
- Slowly bond as they teach each other: he shows her spray paint, breakdancing, headphones; she shows him sacred plants, animal spirits, and how to listen to the jungle.

#### **4. The Bridge**
- Jenga starts painting again, but now inspired by visions and nature.
- His murals start becoming ceremonial objects—symbols that affect reality.
- Ayla begins having stronger visions near his artwork.

#### **5. The Trial**
- The tribe is facing a spiritual crisis—something is out of balance.
- Jenga and Ayla must go on a spirit quest (vision journey) together.
- He faces urban trauma and fear; she faces the seduction of technology and ego.

#### **6. The Merging**
- Jenga realizes his art is a gift passed through his ancestors.
- They both come back changed—Ayla brings new teachings to her people, and Jenga is accepted as a spirit-bridge.
- His art becomes a sacred tool of healing.

---

### **Themes & Symbols:**

- **Graffiti as Sacred Glyphs**  
  His street art evolves into modern petroglyphs, telling stories of spirit animals, ancestors, and energy.

- **Duality & Unity**  
  City vs. jungle, masculine vs. feminine, modern vs. ancient—all balanced in the end.

- **Spiritual Realms**  
  Use visual layers (transparent animals, dreamscapes, bioluminescent plants) to show the spirit world intertwined with ours.

---

### **Visual Style:**

- **Urban sections** – Harsh lines, greyscale with neon accents.
- **Jungle scenes** – Organic textures, lush, alive, flowing patterns.
- **Spiritual visions** – Vibrant, surreal, layered imagery, sacred geometry, glowing lines like auroras.

## 🌿 **Chapter One Outline: “Urban Jungle”**

### **Setting:**  
Inner-city neighborhood; gritty, loud, vibrant with street art and tension. Jenga moves through this world like a shadow.

### **Main Purpose:**  
Establish Jenga’s life, struggles, creative spark, and the event that pushes him toward the jungle.

---

### **Key Scenes:**

1. **Opening Splash Panel:**  
   A sprawling cityscape at sunset, with one rooftop showing Jenga spray-painting a huge, glowing mural. A bird (maybe a hummingbird or hawk—later revealed as a spirit guide) watches from a wire.

2. **Scene 1 – The Tagger’s Path**  
   - Jenga skips school, weaving through alleyways.  
   - Close-ups of his spray can art—sacred-looking glyphs and symbols, although he doesn’t realize their meaning.  
   - He runs from security, narrowly escaping.

3. **Scene 2 – Trouble at Home**  
   - His mom is tired, working late shifts. No father figure present.  
   - Argument ensues: she’s scared for him, he feels misunderstood.

4. **Scene 3 – The Arrest**  
   - He tags a municipal wall and is caught.  
   - He tries to run but gets arrested. The mural he leaves behind seems to shimmer faintly.

5. **Scene 4 – Courtroom Ultimatum**  
   - Judge gives him a choice: juvenile detention or spend the summer with his grandmother in “some jungle nowhere.”  
   - Jenga reacts with sarcasm but is silently curious about the unknown.

6. **Closing Panel**  
   - He sits on the bus or plane, looking out the window, headphones in.  
   - A single panel shows the jungle from above—green and endless.

---

## 🧍‍♂️ **Character Sheet: JENGA (aka Malik or Javier)**

- **Age:** 14–16  
- **Appearance:** Slim build, city-worn clothes, always has spray cans. Afro or twist-out hair, often under a hoodie.  
- **Personality:** Rebellious, sharp-witted, creative. Deeply sensitive beneath his tough shell.  
- **Abilities:** Artistic savant—his tagging isn’t just rebellion; it's ancestral memory made visual.  
- **Flaw:** Trust issues, closed off to guidance.  
- **Arc:** From outsider to spirit-bridge. Learns purpose through pain and connection.

---

## 🧍‍♀️ **Character Sheet: AYLA (or NIA)**

- **Age:** 14–16  
- **Appearance:** Earth-toned clothing, ceremonial jewelry, feathers or beads in hair. Graceful but unpolished.  
- **Personality:** Wise and intuitive, but also curious and playful. She respects the spirit world but questions old traditions.  
- **Abilities:** In training to become a shaman. Sensitive to plant spirits and energy shifts.  
- **Flaw:** Naivety about the outside world; prone to doubting herself.  
- **Arc:** Learns that growth requires balance—between tradition and change.
