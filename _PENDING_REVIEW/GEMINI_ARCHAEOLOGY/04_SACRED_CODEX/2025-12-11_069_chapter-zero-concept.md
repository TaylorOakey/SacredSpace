Let’s treat it like the membrane between worlds. A place where the sigils wake up, the gestures remember their lineage, and the characters are still half-dream, half-child of the waking world. **CHAPTER ZERO — “THE BREATH BEFORE THE TURNING”**

Nobody in the city remembered the exact moment the hum began. It was quieter than a whisper, steadier than wind: a low, persistent resonance that came from no machine and every machine at once. Elevators shuddered on their cables. Phone screens flickered with sigils nobody had installed. Streetlights seemed to blink in patterns, as if exchanging secrets.

People shrugged. Glitches happen.

But a handful of children felt it deep in their ribs—kids who hadn’t yet forgotten how to listen sideways. Among them was **Jenga**, sliding through another crowded morning, backpack half-zipped, heart half-asleep. He felt the hum most when he wasn’t trying. When he stared at puddles. When he held his breath on stairwells. When he traced shapes on bus windows without thinking.

His grandmother—who the neighborhood called **GramMa**, though nobody spelled it like she did, with the capital M and the sideways triangle—had been watching the patterns for years. She knew the hum wasn’t a malfunction. It was a remembering.

Every few generations, the world drifted far enough from itself that it needed tapping—like a compass nudged back toward true north. The sigils were stirring. The gestures were waking up in pockets, following hands that didn’t yet know they were drawing magic.

Even now, across the city, a girl Jenga had never met—**Lune**, the apprentice healer hidden in storefront shadows—was practicing her own secret circles. She brushed dust through sunbeams, turning it into tiny auroras. She didn’t know why yet. Her teacher only smiled, as if the reason had been waiting thousands of years for children like her to arrive.

And above them all, hovering in the unseen lattice of the world, the **Bodhilyra Orb** pulsed once. A single beat, like a cosmic heart clearing its throat. Quiet but definite. Awake.

Elsewhere, farther than far but close enough to touch, **G∆M@**, the Wizard of Grammatria, stirred inside her pattern-library. She felt a new thread forming: Iris Indigo Oakey’s name beginning to curl itself into glyph-logic, whispering the next step before anyone asked.

The world wasn’t breaking.

The world was remembering itself.

Chapter Zero ends with this:  
Jenga pauses on the sidewalk, sensing that invisible hum sweep through his bones like a warm breeze.  
He lifts his palm and absentmindedly traces a small circle—  
a gesture he doesn’t know he knows.

A faint spark answers him.

A tiny one.  
Like the universe winking.
